How to Choose Among Roofing Companies in New Jersey

When evaluating roofing companies New Jersey homeowners should consider, ask these important questions:

  1. Are you properly licensed and insured in New Jersey? Verify current licensing, liability insurance, and workers' compensation coverage.
  2. How long have you been in business? Established companies have proven track records and accountability.
  3. Can you provide local references? Talk to past customers in your area about their experience.
  4. What warranties do you offer? Understand both material and labor warranty coverage.
  5. Will you provide a written estimate? Get detailed, written proposals from multiple companies to compare.
  6. Who will be doing the work? Know if the company uses employees or subcontractors.
  7. What is your project timeline? Understand realistic completion timeframes.
  8. How do you handle permits and inspections? Reputable companies handle all necessary paperwork.

Red Flags to Avoid When Choosing Roofing Companies

Unfortunately, not all roofing companies in NJ operate with integrity. Watch out for these warning signs:

  • Requests for full payment upfront before work begins
  • Door-to-door solicitation after storms (storm chasers)
  • No physical business address or local presence
  • Pressure tactics to sign immediately with "today only" pricing
  • Reluctance to provide proof of insurance or licensing
  • Verbal estimates without written documentation
  • Prices that seem too good to be true (they usually are)
  • No verifiable references or online presence
