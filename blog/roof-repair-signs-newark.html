7 Warning Signs You Need Roof Repair in Newark, NJ

By R&E Roofing January 15, 2025 8 min read

Your roof is your home's first line of defense against the harsh New Jersey weather, from heavy snowstorms to summer thunderstorms. But how do you know when your Newark home needs roof repair? Ignoring the warning signs can lead to costly damage and expensive repairs down the road.

As Newark's trusted roofing experts with over 25 years of experience, we've seen it all. Here are the 7 critical warning signs that indicate your roof needs immediate attention.

1. Missing or Damaged Shingles

After a storm or high winds, take a walk around your property and look up at your roof. Missing shingles are obvious indicators of damage, but damaged shingles can be just as problematic. Look for:

  • Cracked or curled shingles
  • Shingles with granule loss (appearing darker or worn)
  • Torn or loose shingles flapping in the wind
  • Completely missing shingles leaving bare spots

Newark's weather conditions, especially winter ice and summer heat, can cause significant shingle damage. Even a few missing shingles can allow water to penetrate your roof deck, leading to interior damage.

2. Water Stains on Ceilings or Walls

Water stains are a clear sign that moisture is penetrating your roof system. These typically appear as:

  • Brown or yellow discoloration on ceilings
  • Circular stains that grow larger over time
  • Peeling paint or bubbling wallpaper
  • Musty odors in your attic or upper floors

Don't wait to address water stains. What starts as a small leak can quickly escalate into major structural damage, mold growth, and compromised insulation.

3. Sagging Roof Sections

A sagging roof is a serious structural issue that requires immediate professional attention. This indicates that your roof deck or support structure has been weakened, often due to:

  • Long-term water damage
  • Excessive weight from snow accumulation
  • Deteriorated or insufficient support beams
  • Age-related structural degradation

If you notice any sagging, contact R&E Roofing for emergency repair service immediately. This is not a DIY project and requires professional expertise to ensure your home's safety.

4. Granules Accumulating in Gutters

Asphalt shingles are coated with protective granules that shield them from UV rays and weather damage. As shingles age or become damaged, these granules begin to loosen and wash away. Check your gutters and downspouts for:

  • Excessive accumulation of sandy, gritty material
  • Bare spots on shingles (where granules have worn away)
  • Increased granule loss after storms

While some granule loss is normal initially, excessive or accelerated loss indicates your shingles are nearing the end of their lifespan or have sustained damage.

5. Daylight Through Roof Boards

Go into your attic during the day and turn off all lights. If you can see daylight coming through your roof boards, you have a serious problem. This indicates:

  • Gaps in your roofing materials
  • Holes or deterioration in the roof deck
  • Improperly sealed penetrations (vents, chimneys, etc.)

Any opening large enough to let in light is also large enough to let in water, snow, and pests. These gaps need immediate sealing to prevent further damage.

6. Higher Energy Bills

A sudden increase in your heating or cooling costs could indicate poor roof ventilation or insulation issues caused by roof damage. Signs include:

  • Noticeable temperature fluctuations in upper floors
  • Ice dams forming on your roof during winter
  • Excessive heat in your attic during summer
  • Frost or moisture buildup in your attic

Newark homeowners know that proper insulation and ventilation are crucial for managing New Jersey's temperature extremes. Roof damage can compromise these systems, leading to higher energy costs.

7. Your Roof is Over 20 Years Old

Even without visible damage, age alone is a significant factor. Most asphalt shingle roofs last 20-25 years, depending on:

  • Quality of initial installation
  • Type of materials used
  • Weather exposure and maintenance history
  • Ventilation and attic conditions

If your Newark home's roof is approaching or exceeding 20 years old, it's time for a professional inspection. Proactive replacement can prevent emergency situations and give you more control over timing and costs.

Why Newark Homeowners Need to Act Fast

Newark's climate presents unique challenges for roofing systems:

  • Winter weather: Heavy snow loads and ice dams can stress your roof structure
  • Temperature swings: Freeze-thaw cycles cause expansion and contraction, accelerating wear
  • Summer storms: High winds and heavy rain test your roof's integrity
  • Urban environment: Pollution and debris can accelerate shingle deterioration

What to Do If You Notice These Signs

If you've identified one or more of these warning signs, follow these steps:

  1. Document the damage: Take photos for your records and insurance purposes
  2. Call a professional: Contact a licensed, insured roofing contractor for an inspection
  3. Address leaks immediately: Place buckets under active leaks and move valuables
  4. Get multiple opinions: For major repairs, consider getting 2-3 quotes
  5. Don't delay: Small problems become expensive emergencies quickly
