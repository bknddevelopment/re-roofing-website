Common Siding Issues in Cedar Grove:

The wooded character of Cedar Grove neighborhoods creates unique challenges for home exteriors. Overhanging tree branches can scrape against siding during windy conditions, wearing away protective coatings and creating entry points for moisture. During severe storms, falling branches can puncture or severely damage siding panels, requiring immediate repair to prevent water infiltration and interior damage.

Additionally, the temperature extremes of New Jersey winters and summers cause siding materials to expand and contract, potentially leading to cracks, warping, and separation of panels. Older aluminum siding on many Cedar Grove homes is particularly susceptible to denting and corrosion, while original vinyl siding can become brittle with age. Our repair services address these issues before they compromise your home's weather protection and energy efficiency.
